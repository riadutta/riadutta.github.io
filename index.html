Welcome! I study questions in Development and Political Economy with a focus on gender and governance. I routinely incorporate machine learning techniques in my research applying deep learning and structural methods to extract economic insights from unstructured historical and spatial data.

Currently, I am leading a field experiment to study the impact of peer networks among locally elected politicians in Bihar under the guidance of Professor MR Sharan.

Previously I worked as a Senior Research Associate at IMAGO Global Grassroots, with Michael Walton, evaluating the effectiveness of the national anti-poverty program (MGNREGA) on rural women’s collectives using quasi-experimental methods. At J-PAL South Asia, I assessed the outcomes of a gender-inclusive curriculum on adolescent boys and girls with Professor Seema Jayachandran.

Before that at IFMR LEAD, I studied the financial challenges faced by rural women entrepreneurs using primary data and developed an innovative credit scoring model as an alternative to traditional banking systems

I am also the Director of Women in Economics and Policy, an India-based non-profit dedicated to advancing the role of women in economics and allied fields.
