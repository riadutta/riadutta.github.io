About Me

I am a researcher working in Bihar on peer learning among locally elected political leaders, collaborating with Professors MR Sharan, Siddharth George, Siddharth Hari, and Apurva Bamezai. My research interests include Development, Political Economy, Economic History, and Deep Learning.

Previously, I worked as a Senior Research Assistant at IMAGO Global Grassroots, where I evaluated the impact of India's national anti-poverty program (MGNREGA) with Michael Walton. Prior to that, I was a Research Assistant at J-PAL South Asia, working with Professor Seema Jayachandran and Dr. Diva Dhar on a gender-transformative education program in Haryana. Before that, I worked as a Research Assistant at IFMR LEAD, focusing on female labor force participation, labor markets, and entrepreneurship in India.

I am the Director of Women in Economics and Policy, a mentorship platform dedicated to supporting women in these fields.

I hold a Master's degree in Public Policy and Governance from Azim Premji University and a Bachelor's degree in Social Sciences from the Tata Institute of Social Sciences. I am skilled in data analysis using Python, R, and Stata.
