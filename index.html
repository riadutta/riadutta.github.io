Hello! I study questions in Development and Political Economy, with a particular focus on gender and governance.

Recently, I led a field experiment to study the impact of peer networks among locally elected politicians in Bihar under the guidance of Professor MR Sharan.

Previously I worked as a Senior Research Associate at IMAGO Global Grassroots, with Professor Michael Walton, evaluating the effectiveness of the national anti-poverty program (MGNREGA) on rural women’s collectives using quasi-experimental methods. At J-PAL South Asia, I assessed the outcomes of a gender-inclusive curriculum on adolescent boys and girls with Professor Seema Jayachandran.

Before that at IFMR LEAD, I studied the financial challenges faced by rural women entrepreneurs using primary data and helped to develop an innovative credit scoring model as an alternative to traditional banking systems

I also serve as Director of Women in Economics and Policy, a non-profit organization dedicated to advancing women's roles in economics and allied fields.

Women in Econ/Policy

Women in Economics and Policy is an India-based non-profit organisation building a movement to solve the systemic gender diversity problem in economics and policy.

We are a community of over 2000 women across the world in the early or mid stages of their careers. Membership is free and open for all women interested in these fields.

We send members a fortnightly newsletter curating job opportunities, scholarships, and fellowships. Members stay connected via WhatsApp and through local chapters by city and career track, with frequent in-person gatherings. We are also currently working with eight colleges across India to provide resources and mentorship opportunities at scale (funded by the IEA-WE initiative).
